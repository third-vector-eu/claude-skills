# Research Playbook

Web-search strategies for each section of the memo. Use during Phase 1 (Interview), in parallel with questions. Present findings back to the founder; don't silently inject into the memo.

## General principles

- **Triangulate.** Don't trust a single source for market size or competitor pricing. Cross-check 2-3 independent sources.
- **Prefer primary sources.** Company websites, press releases, job postings, product docs, regulatory filings, buyer forums (Reddit, Hacker News, G2, Gartner Peer Insights). Avoid blog-post-of-a-blog-post chains.
- **Date-stamp everything.** In the 2020s, a market report from three years ago is stale. Flag when you're citing something older than 18 months.
- **Be honest about gaps.** If you searched and didn't find reliable data, say so. Don't fill the vacuum with confident-sounding estimates.

## Per-section search strategies

### Problem & target customer

- Search forums where the target ICP gathers: relevant subreddits, industry Slacks, Hacker News threads, LinkedIn groups, Indie Hackers.
- Query: `"[role or title] frustrated with [current tool]"`, `"[ICP] biggest pain"`, `"[industry] workflow problems"`.
- Goal: find real unprompted quotes from the target persona describing the problem.

### Why now

- Query: news from the last 18 months. `"[industry] 2025"`, `"[technology category] regulation"`, `"[buyer type] budget increases"`.
- Check tech inflection markers: benchmarks, pricing curves, open-source release timings, major model releases.
- Check regulatory sites (EU, US federal and state) for rules taking effect in the last year or next year.

### Solution / wedge

- Search for similar wedges that have been tried before. Even failed attempts can clarify why the founder's approach is different.
- Query: `"[problem statement] + tool"`, `"[industry] + automation"`, `"[use case] + AI"`.
- Look for YC Winter/Summer batch descriptions from the last 2 years — they're a good proxy for "who else is attempting this wedge".

### Founder-market fit

- Only research if validating a claim the founder made (e.g., "I built [system] at [company]"). Don't dig into personal history beyond what's claimed.
- Check LinkedIn, GitHub, past company websites, public talks, Substack, podcasts — only to verify claimed credentials.

### Business model & pricing hypothesis

- Search competitor pricing pages directly. Many don't publish, but look for:
  - G2 pricing sections
  - Reddit threads ("how much does X cost")
  - Public case studies with deal size mentions
  - Public RFP responses or government procurement records
- Triangulate with analyst notes (Gartner, Forrester) if accessible.

### Go-to-market motion

- Check competitor career pages for GTM hires (signals of motion shape).
- Search for each competitor on LinkedIn Sales Navigator or similar — what roles are they hiring for?
- Look at review sites (G2, Capterra, TrustRadius) for "how did you discover this product?" signals.
- Check whether they're sponsoring podcasts, running events, or investing in content.

### Market & competition

- Use Crunchbase / Dealroom / PitchBook summaries (free tier) to find:
  - Direct competitors and their last funding round (size, date, investors).
  - M&A activity in the category.
  - Employee count and growth rate (proxy for traction).
- Cross-check with G2 / Capterra category pages for the full competitor list.
- For market size, prefer:
  - Public reports from credible firms (IDC, Gartner, McKinsey), but scrutinise assumptions.
  - Bottoms-up: count of target accounts (e.g., "number of SaaS companies with >100 CX agents") × ACV.
  - Adjacent category sizes as sanity checks.

### Customer discovery status

- No external research for this section. The founder's own conversations are the ground truth.
- If the founder hasn't done customer discovery yet, flag it — don't try to simulate it.

### Risks, unknowns & falsification criteria

- Search for cautionary tales: companies that tried this space and failed. Why did they fail?
- Query: `"[category] + shutdown"`, `"[competitor name] + failed"`, `"post-mortem + [industry]"`.
- Look at regulatory and macro risks: what could change in the next 2 years that would break the thesis?

### Milestones & first experiments

- No external research. This is internal planning.

### The ask

- If the founder is considering fundraising, check the current funding environment for their category — recent deal sizes, active investors, stage norms.
- Query: `"[category] + seed round 2025"`, `"investors + [vertical]"`, `"[region] VC + [stage]"`.

---

## How to present findings

When research surfaces something relevant, show it to the founder like this:

> **Found during research:** [fact + source + date]
>
> **How I'd use this in the memo:** [suggestion].
>
> **Question for you:** does this match what you're seeing? Does it change anything?

Let the founder decide whether and how to incorporate. Research informs the memo; it doesn't overwrite the founder's perspective.
