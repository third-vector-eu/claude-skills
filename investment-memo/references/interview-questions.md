# Interview Questions

The question bank for Phase 1. Use these as prompts — not a rigid script. Follow the founder's energy, but ensure every section gets real coverage before moving on.

## How to use this file

- **Read the core questions for each section.** These are baseline.
- **Add mode-specific questions** based on the founder's calibration result (Domain Expert / Adjacent / Explorer).
- **Do not ask all questions in one go.** Ask 1-2, let the founder talk, ask follow-ups, move on. A memo interview isn't a checklist exercise.
- **Always reflect back** what you heard in 2-3 sentences before moving to the next section. This catches misunderstandings.

---

## Section 1: Problem & target customer

**Core:**
- Who specifically has this problem? Describe them — role, company stage, industry, personality type.
- Walk me through a concrete moment when this problem showed up. What did they do? What did they feel?
- How often does this happen to them? Daily, weekly, quarterly?
- How do they solve it today? What tools, hacks, workarounds, or manual processes?
- What does it cost them when they don't solve it — dollars, hours, lost deals, lost sleep?
- Why hasn't this already been solved?

**Domain Expert additions:**
- What's the most common misdiagnosis of this problem? What do outsiders get wrong?
- What's your contrarian view on who the real buyer is?

**Explorer additions:**
- What gave you the idea that this was a problem worth solving? How confident are you that this is real?
- How many people outside your immediate circle have you talked to about this?

---

## Section 2: Why now

**Core:**
- What has changed in the last 1-3 years that makes this urgent or possible now?
- Is the "why now" on the supply side (new capability), demand side (new buyer behaviour), or both?
- What evidence do you see of that change in the real world — news, job postings, funding rounds, customer quotes?
- If this why-now was also true in 2022, then it's not one. Can you sharpen it?

**Domain Expert additions:**
- What's the specific inflection in your market that most people in it haven't noticed yet?
- Who else is noticing? Are competitors moving? Are buyers' budgets shifting?

**Explorer additions:**
- What signals have you personally seen, not just read about?
- Is there a chance this trend is already plateauing or backlashing?

---

## Section 3: Solution / wedge

**Core:**
- In one sentence — what does the first product do?
- What's the narrowest possible version that's still useful?
- What does a first customer get on day one?
- What's NOT in the wedge? What are you explicitly choosing not to build first?
- Why this wedge instead of a different entry point?
- Where does the wedge lead over the next 3 years?

**Domain Expert additions:**
- What's the version of this product that you've seen built badly before? What did they get wrong?
- What's the "obvious next step" in your industry that most founders would take but is actually wrong? Why are you not taking it?

**Explorer additions:**
- How do you know the wedge is narrow enough? Have you tried describing it to someone in the industry?
- What's your biggest risk that the wedge is solving a pain point that doesn't actually exist?

---

## Section 4: Founder-market fit

**Core:**
- What in your background makes you a credible builder of this specifically?
- What have you seen that others haven't?
- What's your unfair advantage — network, technical skill, scar tissue, privileged access?
- What will you need to hire or learn or partner for?
- Why are you the one doing this and not someone better-positioned?

**Domain Expert additions:**
- What's the skill or perspective you have that's rare in this market? Not "I worked here a long time" — the specific thing.
- Are you a plausible *winner* of this market, or a plausible *builder*? What would turn you from the second into the first?

**Explorer additions:**
- You don't have domain depth yet. What's your plan to build it — through hires, advisors, customer conversations, or immersion?
- Who in your network is the closest you have to a domain expert, and what did they say about the idea?

---

## Section 5: Business model & pricing hypothesis

**Core:**
- Who holds the budget for this?
- What is the unit of value you're charging for — seats, usage, outcomes, something else?
- What ACV are you targeting at first? At scale?
- What did you use to triangulate that number — comparables, cost structure, willingness-to-pay data?
- Monthly, annual, or multi-year contract?
- What does gross margin look like on a deal?

**Domain Expert additions:**
- What's the pricing model your industry defaults to, and should you break from it? Why or why not?
- Where's the pricing power? (Is it switching-cost-driven, performance-driven, ecosystem-driven, brand-driven?)

**Explorer additions:**
- Have you tested any pricing anchors with real people? What happened?
- What's your riskiest pricing assumption?

---

## Section 6: Go-to-market motion

**Core:**
- Who are your first 10 customers — name them if you can?
- How are you reaching them? Cold? Warm? Community? Events? Partnerships?
- What's the message that gets them on a call?
- What's the motion for customers 11-50? Does anything change?
- What's the sales motion — self-serve, inside sales, field?
- What have you already done that worked? What have you tried that didn't?

**Domain Expert additions:**
- What's the GTM motion everyone else in your category uses, and why are you doing something different?
- Who owns this buyer's attention today — what does your wedge need to displace?

**Explorer additions:**
- Have you personally tried to sell or demo this yet? What happened?
- What's your biggest unknown in the GTM motion right now?

---

## Section 7: Market & competition

**Core:**
- How big is this market? Walk me through your math.
- Who are the top 3 direct competitors? What do they do well? What do they miss?
- Who are the indirect competitors — tools, workarounds, internal builds?
- Why hasn't a big incumbent already solved this?
- What's your contrarian view of this market — something most people in it don't believe?

**Domain Expert additions:**
- Steelman the biggest incumbent's response when they notice you. What do they do?
- Who are the 1-2 competitors you respect most, and what makes them dangerous?

**Explorer additions:**
- Are you sure you've found all the competitors? Have you looked for indirect ones — consultants, agencies, in-house teams?
- Is this market growing or shrinking? What's your evidence?

---

## Section 8: Customer discovery status

**Core:**
- How many customer conversations have you had?
- What types — cold outreach calls, warm intros, paying customers?
- What's the single most surprising thing you heard?
- What's the single most painful "no" you've heard, and do you believe it?
- Where are your conversations thin — which segments haven't you probed yet?
- What's your conversion signal so far — waitlist signups, LOIs, pilot agreements, revenue?

(Same questions across all modes — but in Explorer mode, push harder on "have you actually had real conversations yet?" vs. imagined customers.)

---

## Section 9: Risks, unknowns & falsification criteria

**Core:**
- What would have to be true for this business to fail? Top 3 risks.
- For each risk: what specifically would make you walk away from this idea?
- What's the cheapest experiment that would stress-test your biggest bet?
- What's your "kill criterion" — the moment at which you'd stop?

**Domain Expert additions:**
- What's the risk that someone new to the market would see but that you might be blind to?
- Where's your conviction strongest, and might that itself be a risk?

**Explorer additions:**
- What's the one thing that could go wrong that you haven't yet stress-tested?
- If someone much smarter than you spent an hour critiquing this, what would they say?

---

## Section 10: Milestones & first experiments

**Core:**
- What are the 3 things you'll do in the next 30 days?
- What do each of those experiments test, and what does success look like?
- Where will you be in 90 days if all goes well? If it doesn't?
- What decision are you making at the 90-day mark?

---

## Section 11: The ask

**Core:**
- Are you raising capital, bootstrapping, or somewhere in between?
- If raising: how much, for what, and on what timeline?
- What 1-2 hires would most change the trajectory?
- Who are the 3-5 ideal early customers you'd want introductions to?
- What other support — advisors, partners, expertise — would be high-leverage?

**Bootstrapping-specific addition:**
- Why is bootstrapping the right choice for this specific business (not just a default)?
- What's the revenue milestone that would let you hire #1?

---

## Meta-questions (ask once, near the end of the interview)

- What parts of this memo do you feel strongest about?
- What parts are you secretly worried about?
- If you had to pick one assumption in this whole thing that, if wrong, would break everything — what is it?
- Who have you shown your thinking to, and what did they push back on?
